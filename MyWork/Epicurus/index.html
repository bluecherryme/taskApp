EPICURUS

– A Greek Philosopher –

"The art of living well and the art of dying well are one."

His Life

It's been a while since he has been around. Epicurus lived from 341 to 270 B.C. To set that into perspective, Aristotle, the last of the great three to die, died 322 B.C.

  • Born on the Island of Samos
  • Before 18: Studied 4 years philosophy under the Platonist Pamphilus
  • 18-20 years: 2 year military service in Athens
  • 20 years: moved to what is now the Turkish Coast
  • 35 years: moved to Athens where he stayed for the rest of his life and founded a philosophy school in his garden called “The Garden”
  • 72 years: Death

His teachings

Epicurus appreciated the good things in life and encouraged his followers to focus on those, rather than dwell on problems, aches and misfortunes. The absence of evil is good.

He held human friendships at high value as a source of generating happiness and satisfaction.

He did however not believe that happiness could be found in over-consumption of delicious foods, amorous love or drugs as that would eventually only lead to suffering.

He encouraged people not to fear death as he did not believe in life after death. One would not be aware of being dead and had therefore nothing to fear.
